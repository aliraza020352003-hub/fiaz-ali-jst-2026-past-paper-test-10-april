READING COMPREHENSION (Official Text)

"I Have a Dream" is a public speech that was delivered by American civil rights activist Martin Luther King Jr. during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom on August 28, 1963. In the speech, King called for civil and economic rights and an end to racism in the United States. Delivered to over 250,000 civil rights supporters from the steps of the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C., the speech was a defining moment of the civil rights movement and among the most iconic speeches in American history.

King's vision was a country where his children would one day live in a nation where they would not be judged by the color of their skin but by the content of their character. He emphasized that the struggle for liberty and equality must be met with dignity and discipline, avoiding physical violence but maintaining a relentless pursuit of justice for all citizens, regardless of their background.

    Reading Comprehension (Read carefully before starting):

    Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.’s ‘I Have a Dream’ speech was a defining moment in the American Civil Rights Movement. In the first part of his speech, he painted a somber picture of the conditions faced by African Americans, highlighting the deep-seated deprivation of their basic human rights and the broken promise of the Emancipation Proclamation. Moving into the second part, his tone shifted towards a powerful vision of the future—one built on the pillars of freedom, equality, and peace for all citizens regardless of race. He emphasized that this change must be achieved through non-violent struggle, warning that any call to violence would only result in chaos and a loss of public support, ultimately damaging the credibility of their cause. The speech concluded with a universal plea for human rights to be recognized not just locally or nationally, but globally.
